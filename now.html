Now

This is a Now Page. A concept seemingly invented by Derek Sivers that's meant to showcase what you're doing right now. Kinda self-explanatory, but a neat idea! I will update this page when I feel like it, but hopefully pretty often.

Thinking

I’m a recent fan of the Tumblr account Forgotten Flickr, which curates and shares old, abandoned photos. Many of my favorites are from the mid 2000s. A lot of them are very mundane, taken by someone who probably just got a digital camera. Lots of photos of living spaces, sunsets, and cats. It’s a real trip to browse this blog and take a raw look into the recent past. Things looked so different just a few years ago. It’s wild and sad and sweet and beautiful. Highly recommended!

This has got me thinking of one of my personal regrets: that I have no photos of my childhood bedroom. I remember bits and pieces, but no longer have a mental image of the entire room and how it all fit together. It’s real easy to take your surroundings for granted and not realize how attached you are to the places you regularly inhabit. And when you move on and enough time passes, it’d be nice to have a photo or two that you could refer back to. Something to fan the embers of memory.

So anyway, I’m gonna try to take more photos of people and places and things and I think you should, too.
