Now

This is a Now Page. A concept seemingly invented by Derek Sivers that's meant to showcase what you're doing right now. Kinda self-explanatory, but a neat idea! I will update this page when I feel like it, but hopefully pretty often. There's also an RSS feed, if you're into that sort of thing.

2024.09.14 – What's Next?

Cohost is shutting down soon and I’m scrambling to figure out what my online life is going to look like in the aftermath. It’s been the only social media I’ve used regularly for the last two years, so there’s going to be a void. It was a nice place! It let me stay in touch with a few good friends and meet a bunch of new people as well as see a bunch of good posts. It was also nice to have a place to go and say “hey, check out this new thing I made” on occasion. And it was (almost) the only place to hang out online without being manipulated and marketed to by actually-evil corporations. Now that’s all going away and it’s a bummer.

But this pending change is also very motivating, which is the silver lining I’m trying to focus on. It’s making me finally get an RSS reader and create my own feed, so I can keep in touch with some of the aforementioned folks as well as occasionally shout into a void. It’s reignited my (frequent) desire to redesign my own website and continue to encourage others to have personal websites. And from what I’m seeing, most of the other users feel similarly, which is extremely encouraging. Another indie web boom in the making, at least when it comes to my dwindling circle of internet friends and mutuals.

My main goal in the coming weeks is to clean up my website so it better allows for “posts” and serves as a little more than a graphic design portfolio. I’ve kinda painted myself into a corner by relying on this Now page. It’s a corner I still really like. But I dunno. I might change it up. I love Will’s website and might pivot to doing something more like that. Maybe using Jack’s Free Website as a starting point.

I’m also gonna get real into RSS and treat that as if it’s a social media platform. People are doing cool stuff with RSS, like Ruby’s “rosting” concept. You can just make posts that only exist in the RSS feed. It's neat! There's potential here. It's like tweets without the interactions. I’ve made a few rosts myself.

And lastly, I’m going to spend more time in forums. We’ve got Famiclub which is criminally underused (even by me) and has a lot of potential. I’ve also had a nice time visiting the Insert Credit forum in the past. And there’s also the Last Words Book Club, which is my fledgling Discord server for horror book discussions. (Not really a forum, but you knowhatimean.) These are nice spaces!

Also, I recognize that I have the privilege of not needing to rely on social media to foster a following and generate income. I have a job that I like and it’s (seemingly) pretty secure. And I also realize that I have the free time and the knowledge to go off the beaten path like this without much consequence. So it’s a blessing to not be tempted by Bluesky, Instagram, etc. and whatever is happening inside those walled gardens.

All that could change someday. (Although I have sworn an oath to never join Bluesky.) But right now, I have the ability to say “fuck that shit.” And let me tell you, it feels nice! I’m not going to participate in those spaces unless I get incredibly desperate. And maybe not even then, because I have generally had a Bad Time and not much to show for it, tbh.

I'm bummed by the last Good Website going away, but I'm also a little optimistic about my evolving relationship with the internet. Being online without being Online™. Fewer distractions. More intentionality. Things are clunky out here, but the occasional speed bump also has its uses.

That’s all for now. Gonna go play some Elden Ring. Thanks for reading. Good luck out there.
